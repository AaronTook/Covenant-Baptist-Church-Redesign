Church Ministries
Sunday School

Our Sunday School hour is an important, time honored tradition that allows children, youth, and adults to take a deeper look at God’s Word and learn how it applies to our everyday lives. We offer classes for adults and children of all ages.

Children’s Church

Children ages 4 thru 3rd grade gather for a time of Bible lessons, singing and craft during the Sunday Morning worship hour.

Home Groups

Fellowship and friendships are cultivated at home group meetings, which usually meet once a month. These informal groups provide an environment for discipleship yet allows the freedom for each group to focus on specific needs within the group. Our Home Groups also have the opportunity to do outreach in their neighborhoods as doors open.

Vacation Bible School

Each summer we host a VBS for children ages 3 to 6th grade. Usually conducted in the evening hours, the week not only is filled with fun and excitement for the children, but more importantly provides each child with solid Biblical teaching on a particular theme from God’s Word. The children are also given opportunity to be involved in a special ministry project to our community or a missionary.

Community Outreach
ESL & Citizenship Classes

Interested in learning English? If so, we are here to help. It is an amazing step to leave your home country and start a new life in the USA! Everything is different, and learning a new language can be difficult. In our ESL course, you will learn about U.S. culture and customs, about the local community, and you will be part of a special group to support and encourage you on your everyday journey.
Learn more about ESL here!

Special Projects

Throughout the year we seek opportunities to serve others and share Christ’s love. For example, several times each year we collect donations for the Dayton Gospel Mission to provide for those in need in our own city. One month the women of the church provided baked goods for the Victory Project and have plans to reach out to our local fire and police departments. The congregation was recently given the opportunity to provide critical items needed for the medical clinic at Pacific Garden Mission in Chicago. A team of volunteers traveled to the “Old Lighthouse” to deliver these important items. Of course, the purpose behind all of our efforts in these projects remains the same: that the Gospel of God’s Grace would be clearly communicated to those on the receiving end.
